## Anti-pattern verdict

Pass. The product does not read as generic AI-generated UI. It uses credible native product patterns, restrained color and motion, real reading data, stable cover origins, and no decorative entrance choreography. The CLI detector completed with zero findings. The remaining problems are interaction-detail inconsistencies rather than visual slop.

## Overall impression

The motion system already has a strong quiet/native baseline. Its best moments are the shared-cover reader transition, persistent root continuity, disciplined gesture arbitration, and architectural reduced-motion support. The next improvement should not add more effects. It should shorten routine transitions, unify timing ownership, close modal accessibility gaps, and make small state changes feel causally connected.

Overall cognitive load is low. The main hotspot is Reader Appearance: seven visible actions plus passive scale and brightness indicators, with theme selection duplicated between a menu and Light/Dark preview buttons.

## Strengths

1. Reduced motion is architectural. App and OS preferences converge in `getMotionPolicy`, feed one `MotionConfig`, and are backed by CSS overrides.
2. Gesture ownership is disciplined. Edge Back has directional and threshold claims; sheet gestures arbitrate against scrollable descendants and controls.
3. State motion usually serves continuity. Roots remain mounted, list insertion stagger is limited, counters and bars animate from semantic state, and shared-cover transitions restore focus to the source.
4. Persistent navigation surfaces use `aria-hidden`, `pointer-events: none`, and `inert` together.
5. Touch targets and global focus visibility are generally strong for the iPhone/PWA target.

## Priority issues

### P1 — Generic sheets do not own focus or isolate the background

`MotionSheet` provides dialog semantics, Escape dismissal, and gestures, but does not consistently capture prior focus, place focus inside, trap Tab, or inert the active root. Production inspection of Reader Appearance left focus on `BODY` and the Settings root non-inert. Reading Goal separately implements a focus trap, showing that the shared contract is inconsistent.

Why it matters: keyboard and VoiceOver users can encounter the page underneath a visually modal surface. This is a correctness problem, not cosmetic polish.

Fix: centralize initial focus, Tab cycling, background inert ownership, and post-exit focus restoration in `MotionSheet`; support `initialFocusRef`; remove duplicate one-off behavior from Reading Goal.

Suggested command: `$impeccable harden the shared MotionSheet focus, inert, and restoration contract`

### P2 — Reader close is too cinematic for a daily loop

Reader enter is 460 ms and exit is 340 ms, while content exit also inherits an approximately 110 ms delay. The practical close can take roughly 450 ms before completion and source-focus restoration.

Why it matters: opening may deserve a branded transition, but closing a book is a repeated utility action. The current ending feels slow, then Library progress updates abruptly.

Fix: use explicit enter and exit variants. Target about 280–320 ms for opening, 200–240 ms for closing, and remove the exit delay while preserving shared-cover geometry.

Suggested command: `$impeccable animate the reader transition with asymmetric fast-exit timing`

### P2 — Motion timing has competing sources of truth

TypeScript tokens, CSS variables, reader-control constants, edge-settle constants, and duplicated EPUB/page swipe-settle logic own overlapping timings. The live feel is close today, but drift is likely as states expand.

Why it matters: inconsistent timing is perceived as small friction everywhere and becomes difficult to tune safely.

Fix: define one role table for press, local state, tab, push, sheet, reader, gesture settle, and reduced crossfade. Generate or test CSS parity. Consolidate the duplicated `--reader-swipe-duration` ownership in `EpubReader.tsx` and `page.tsx`.

Suggested command: `$impeccable normalize motion timing roles and remove duplicate ownership`

### P2 — Reader-return progress lacks continuity

The Reading dashboard progress track animates, while Library featured and list-row progress widths jump. After a smooth shared-cover return, the changed progress value appears abruptly.

Why it matters: the saved-progress update is the natural completion signal for the reading loop.

Fix: animate Library progress width over the standard 190–200 ms local-state duration, beginning as reader exit completes; retain an instant or reduced crossfade path for reduced motion.

Suggested command: `$impeccable animate Library progress as the reader-close completion cue`

### P2 — Auto-hidden reader controls are not discoverable on first use

The persistent menu retains a 48 px hit target, but its visible material collapses to a narrow sliver; the icon becomes 38% opaque, shifts, and scales down. There is no first-use explanation.

Why it matters: experienced users enjoy the quiet canvas, while first-time users may think controls disappeared.

Fix: keep the control expanded until the first explicit toggle, or briefly show a static “更多选项” chip after the first auto-hide. Do not pulse; give reduced-motion users the same informational cue without animation.

Suggested command: `$impeccable onboard the reader control affordance without adding decorative motion`

## Persona red flags

- First-time reader: the collapsed reader-control sliver and edge Back are hard to discover; Reader Appearance presents overlapping theme choices.
- Daily reader: persistent state and shared-cover continuity are excellent, but the approximately 450 ms close adds repeated friction.
- Reduced-motion or VoiceOver user: reduced-motion handling is strong, but generic sheet focus and background isolation are a serious gap. Animated totals are labelled but are not live-region announcements.

## Minor observations

- Reader controls remain non-inert until their hide animation completes, leaving a brief interval where departing controls may still be focusable.
- Sheet backdrop opacity has more than one owner during entrance and drag; one owner would produce cleaner continuity.
- A 35 ms stagger on a single reader toolbar cluster may be less native than simultaneous reveal.
- Some reduced-motion paths use a 120 ms crossfade. This is acceptable, but should be checked against the intended quiet/native feel.
- Physical iPhone Safari remains the meaningful final frame-pacing check; background Chrome throttled live animation sampling.

## Emotional journey

- Entry is calm and clear, though the empty Library and Reading roots are similarly functional rather than rewarding.
- The strongest peak is the shared-cover transition into the reader and exact-source focus restoration.
- The main valley is the auto-hidden control affordance, which becomes too subtle before the user has learned it.
- The ending should become a shorter close followed by a gentle progress-settle moment: a quiet confirmation that the reading position was saved.
