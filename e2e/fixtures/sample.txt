Native Navigation Sample

Chapter One

The first page begins with a quiet room, a long table, and a book left open near the window. The reader returns to the same line after every interruption.

Navigation should preserve place. A temporary screen may cover the library, but it must never destroy the source view or move its scroll position.

The book opens from its cover. The cover expands toward the reading surface while the surrounding interface yields without flashing or changing theme.

Closing the reader reverses that relationship. Focus returns to the source cover so keyboard and assistive technology users do not lose their place.

Chapter Two

A pushed screen enters from the right. The existing root shifts only enough to preserve spatial context, then waits behind the active surface.

The visible back button and the left-edge gesture share one navigation action. Browser Back follows the same state history.

A short edge drag returns to its starting position. A committed edge drag removes exactly one pushed route and exposes the preserved root.

Vertical scrolling near the left edge remains vertical. Horizontal reading gestures belong to the reader and never leak into application navigation.

Chapter Three

Sheets rise from the bottom with a dimmed background. Their content remains interruptible while opening and closing, including rapid repeated input.

The table of contents, reader settings, custom reader settings, Ask AI, reading goal, book actions, delete confirmation, and group tools use one sheet layer.

The Ask AI conversation scrolls independently. Its composer stays at the bottom when the viewport becomes shorter and when the input receives focus.

Closing a sheet returns focus to the command that opened it. Escape and browser Back dismiss the same topmost sheet state.

Chapter Four

The library keeps every book available offline. Imported bytes remain in IndexedDB and are reopened from durable data instead of an expired object URL.

Reading progress belongs to the full book. The visible page label updates with movement and never confuses a chapter page with total-book progress.

The reading surface hides native scrollbars while retaining touch scrolling. Content remains readable in light, dark, and custom background modes.

Ambient color comes from the active cover. It crossfades as state changes and does not obscure foreground text.

Chapter Five

Motion is functional feedback. Press states begin immediately, transitions preserve hierarchy, and completion settles without an extra bounce.

Reduced motion keeps every operation available. It removes travel and scale while retaining short opacity changes that communicate state.

Numbers interpolate only when values change. New library items enter in a small stagger, while existing items never replay their entrance during filtering.

Streaming text appears continuously. Completed messages enter once and remain stable during later conversation updates.

Chapter Six

Performance is part of interaction quality. A normal transition should produce enough animation frames to look continuous on a mobile display.

One delayed frame may occur under load, but long main-thread blocks must not become the normal shape of a navigation transition.

Stable dimensions prevent controls from shifting when labels, counters, and progress values update. Touch targets remain reachable at every supported width.

The final page closes the sample with the same rule as the first: preserve context, preserve control, and make every transition explain where the interface went.
